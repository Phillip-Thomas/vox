Direction A: Material Families
Thesis: Each block family gets its own shader vocabulary while staying inside the same PBR/triplanar pipeline.
- Sand/dirt: wind-carried dust and dry top-surface wisps.
- Lava/basalt: boiling thermal cells, cooling crust, ash, and embers.
- Ice/crystal: frost wind, facets, internal glow, and glints.
- Wood/ores: grain, knots, veining, and metal catchlights.

Direction B: Reality Resolution Stages
Thesis: Treat the renderer as a story system. The same shader supports early flat reality, first material awakening, living world fidelity, and later paradox intensity.
- `bare`: monochrome unresolved block shading with no procedural detail.
- `color`: colored blocks with no procedural detail.
- `material`: partial material/hazard cues.
- `alive`: current production look.
- `paradox`: heightened later-dimension look.

Selected:
Blend A and B. Authored material families provide the visual richness; reality stages provide the plot mechanic.

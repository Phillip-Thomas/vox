# Design Directions

## Direction A: Tiny Grazing Herds

Sparse groups of readable quadrupeds with bobbing heads, slow gait loops, small tails, and occasional woolly bodies. Best for a calm living-world feel, but it risks looking too pastoral if all species share the same silhouette.

## Direction B: Biome Critter Ecology

Separate archetypes by material and climate: grazers, woollies, runners, and hoppers. Grass and lush dirt get fuller herbivores; sand/arid dirt gets smaller quick silhouettes. Motion stays lightweight through per-vertex shader attributes.

## Direction C: Spectral Fauna Accents

Fewer animals, stronger alien tinting, more reality-stage shimmer, and higher emphasis on rendering-progression effects. Strong atmosphere, but too magical for the first fauna foundation and less useful as a general ecology layer.

Status: complete.

- The strongest visual lift came from treating foliage as coherent tufts, not simply increasing card count.
- Per-tuft tint is important because per-card randomness turns dense canopies into noisy static.
- One-branch whorls are too sparse for this game's camera scale; two-to-three whorls work better as the baseline.
- Small procedural skeletons need a compensation path. Boosting cards per sparse leaf-bearing node improves edge cases without globally raising every tree's cost.
- The `tree-test.html` summary hook is valuable as rendered proof when shader keys, custom attributes, and species controls need to be validated.
